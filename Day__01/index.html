How internet works ?

SIM card:---

A tiny chip inside your phone that identifies you to a telecom company, like an ID card.
    It's how Jio/Airtel/Vi know "this phone belongs to this customer."

Telecom Company / ISP (Internet Service Provider):---

A company that owns physical infrastructures (tower,cables,equipment) and sell you access to the internet.
    Jio, Airtel, Vi, BSNL, ACT Fibernet - these are all ISPs.
    This is the company you are actually paying when you recharge.
                          Telecom Company
                    JIO, AIRTEL, VI, BSNL, VODA, OTHERS
                          infrastructure
                      Mobile towers, cables

Mobile tower:---

those tall metal/antenna structures you see around your city.
    Your phone talks to the nearest tower wirelessely using radio waves
    the same basic idea as a walkie-talkie, just far more advanced.

Data:---

the actual information travelling back and forth: a webpage, a video frame, a whatsapp message.
    Measured in MB(megabytes) and GB(gigabytes). When your recharge plan say "2GB/day," thats the amount of data
    you are allowed to send/receive that day before the compaqny slows you down or charges extra.

Recharge:---

literally a payment to your ISP for a fixed amount of data + calling minutes, valid for a fixed number of days.
    You are not buying internet out of thin air --- you are paying a real company that owns real physical equipment to let 
    your phone use their network.

Broadband:---

a wired (cable/fibre) internet connection straight into your house, instead of going through a mobile tower.
    Usually used for home wifi.

Router / Modem:---

the box at home(often with blinking lights) that takes the broadband connection coming into your house
    and converts it into a WIFI signal your phone/laptop can connect to.

Network:---

two or more computers connected so they can talk to each other.Your home WIFI is a small network. 
    Your college's WIFI is a bigger network

4 Types of network:---

1. PAN
    2. LAN
    3. MAN
    4. WAN

PAN:---

the smallest possible network, covering just a few meters around one person.
    Example:- your phone connected to Bluetooth earphones, or a smartwatch synced to your phone.

LAN:---

devices connected within one limited physical place: one house, one classroom, one office floor, one school building.
    Example: every phone/laptop connected to your home WIFI router is one LAN.
    Your school's computer lab, all wired together, is also a LAN.
    Range: roughly one building, often under 1km.

MAN:---

multiple LANs connected together across a whole city or a large campus with many buildings.
    Example: a cable TV company's network covering an entire city;
    a university connecting its 10 different department buildings together;
    a city wide public WIFI project

WAN:---

a network spanning a Huge area: multiple cities, states, countries, or even the entire planet.
    Example: your ISP's nation wide backbone connecting every city it serves is a WAN

Client Server Model:---

Client:---

the one who asks for something. Your browser (Chrome, Safari, etc) is a client.
    Your phone's Instagram app is a client.

Server:---

the one who has the things and send it back. A server is just a powerful computer that sits "always on"
    waiting for requests, somewhere in a data center.

Request:---

the message the client sends ("hey, give me the youtube homepage".)

Response:---

the message the server sends back (the actual HTML/video/data).

IP Address:---

Internet Protocol Address---> a unique number assigned to every device connected to the internet, 
    so other devices known where to send data,
    LOOKS LIKE____ 142.250.183.78
    Open cmd, type ipconfig

DNS:---

Domain Name System___ a gaint, distributed phonebook that converts a domain name
    like( youTube.com) into the actual IP address of the server that owns it.

Domain name:---

the human-readable website name, like amazon.com

Domain Name Server:---

a special server whose entire job is to answer the question 
    "what IP address does this domain point to ?"

Walk through the actual sequence on the board, step by step like a story:

You type youTube.com and hit Enter.
    Your browser asks a DNS server: "What's the IP address for youtube.com ?"
    DNS replies: "It's 142.250.183.78."
    Now your browser knows exactly which computer on Earth to talk to.

IPv4:---

Internet Protocol version 4__ numbers(each 0-255) separated by dots, like 142.250.183.78.
    Because each number is built from 32 binary bits total, IPv4 can only create about
     4.3 billion unique addresses in total.

     32 bit

IPv6:---

the newer format built specifically to solve that shortage. It looks completely different
    longer, and uses letters and colons instead of just dots,
    like__ 2401.4900.1c20.0000.0000.0000.1a2b

    128 bit Hexadecimal
